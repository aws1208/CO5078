Winter Comes to Manchester

Deansgate, the new King’s Landing

Imagine the Seven Kingdoms stretched across the streets of Manchester: the Northern Quarter as a haven for free folk, Castlefield as the rebuilt Winterfell, and Old Trafford looming like a modern Dragonpit. Rival houses vie for control of the Metrolink, the canals, and the beating heart of the city.

This fan-made setting reimagines iconic Game of Thrones locations in familiar Mancunian landmarks—rain-soaked cobbles, red-brick strongholds, and skies that always feel a little bit like winter.

Great Houses of Manchester

  • House Stark of Castlefield
    “The Rain Is Coming.”
    Guardians of the canals and old mills, House Stark keeps watch over the city’s historic heart, loyal and stubborn as the stone arches that frame their domain.
  • House Lannister of Spinningfields
    “Debts Paid in Gold and Glass.”
    Dwelling in the gleaming towers of finance, they rule over coin, contracts, and quiet threats whispered in rooftop bars.
  • House Targaryen of Old Trafford
    “Fire, Blood, and Added Time.”
    Their banners fly red over roaring crowds. Legends speak of three “dragons” once dominating the pitch, now waiting to rise again.
  • House Greyjoy of Salford Quays
    “We Do Not Commute.”
    Masters of the waterways and MediaCity, they sail trams and ferries alike, claiming every dock and pier as their own.

Whispers from the Northern Quarter

Year 1 – The Gathering Storm

Rumours spread of a mysterious chill rolling in from the Pennines, as street artists paint white walkers on shuttered doors.

Year 2 – Battle of Piccadilly

Rival banners clash over control of the station—whoever commands the trains commands the realm.

Year 3 – The Metrolink Accord

A fragile peace is signed on a tram between Altrincham and Bury, though no one expects it to last the full journey.
